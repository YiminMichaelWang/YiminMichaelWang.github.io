AI can only be trusted when its builders can be trusted.

I am Yimin (Michael) Wang, an incoming Ph.D. student in the College of Information Sciences and Technology at Penn State University, beginning in Fall 2026. I completed my B.S. in Computer Science and B.S. in Applied Statistics at Wake Forest University. My current research centers on interpretability for large language models and AI systems, especially efficient attribution methods that help explain how models use prompts, data, and internal mechanisms to produce decisions. I was advised as an undergraduate by Dr. Fan Yang at Wake Forest University, and I will begin my doctoral research with Dr. Minhao Cheng at Penn State.

Research direction

From individual data points to model behavior.

I study how data, tokens, pixels, graph structures, and model components contribute to AI decisions. The long-term goal is practical interpretability: explanations that are mathematically principled, computationally efficient, and useful to scientists.
